Professor Kamal Merit Awards
For Young Researchers

(for Discovery and Documentation of Indian Fungi)



Brief Biodata
Professor Kamal, born on September 2, 1941, in Kushinagar District, overcame his modest background to achieve academic excellence. He pursued his education at Uditnarayan Kshatriya Inter College before earning his bachelor's and master's degrees from the University of Gorakhpur. His research journey began at Gorakhpur University under Professor K.S. Bhargava, leading to his Ph.D. in Botany. Joining the university as a lecturer in 1969, he dedicated his career to mycological research, focusing on soil microbiology, aeromycology, and plant pathogenic fungi. His work extended across India and into Nepal, earning him advanced training at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology under Prof. Emil Muller. He held esteemed fellowships, including at Tribhuvan University in Nepal and Dr. H.S. Gaur University, and actively contributed to scientific conferences and organizations, serving as President of the Mid-Eastern Zone of the Indian Phytopathological Society and Vice-President of the Mycological Society of India. With over 250 research papers, Professor Kamal significantly advanced the taxonomy of foliar fungi, discovering more than 200 new species and several new genera. His work appeared in prestigious international journals, and he collaborated with renowned mycologists worldwide. As a mentor, he guided over 60 Ph.D. students and co-authored four books, along with a monograph on Indian cercosporoid fungi. Respected for his integrity, wisdom, and dedication, he remained an influential educator and researcher. His perseverance and intellectual contributions, despite his humble beginnings, left a lasting legacy in mycology.

Award Instituted by: Dr. Sanjay Singh, Scientist-F, National Fungal Culture Collection of India (NFCCI), Agharkar Research Institute, Pune, Maharashtra

Criteria:
Taxonomy and systematics of fungi are dynamic and rising field, but declining interests in these fundamental aspects are the matter of great concern in modern era require utmost attention of mycological fraternity of India. New generation of budding researchers are encouraged to make concerted efforts in discovering and studying fungi from various natural sources for their various applications in applied disciplines. It is important to establish correct identity/ validation of identity by combination of morphotaxonomy and multigene phylogeny of newly discovered as well as known India taxa of fungi. This would enable subsequent generations to continue honoring and appreciating the fundamental mycological science as well as exceptional educator-cum researcher, Prof. Kamal.

Eligibility:
Young researchers and teachers with age not exceeding 40 years shall be eligible for this award. Candidate publishing original research papers in referred journal of repute on bi-phasic taxonomy of microfungi in general or on specialized group like cercosporoid fungi shall be given preference Along with fungal bi-phasic taxonomy, work on applied research will also be considered for the award Undergraduate and post graduate students are not eligible for this award.

Terms & Conditions for Award :

> The award will be given by the Association of Fungal Biologists during its annual meet and conference every year

> Award will be given to one best oral and best poster presentations. Awards for oral and poster presentations will be given separately.

> The oral and poster presentations will be delivered during annual meeting and associated symposium/conference of AFB

> ORAL PRESENTATION: Young faculty/Post Doc Fellows (not exceeding 40 years of age) engaged in mycological research/teaching are eligible for this award (First & Second)

> POSTER PRESENTATION: Young faculty/Post Doc Fellows (not exceeding 40 years of age) engaged in mycological research/teaching are eligible for this award (First & Second)

> A committee constituted by the AFB will screen the applicants for the best poster and oral presentation award.

> Preference would be given to quality work on the biphasic taxonomy and systematics of complex / rare/ interesting/ group of micro-macrofungi.

> Each awards will carry a memento and a merit certificate/citation.
> No cash prize will be given
